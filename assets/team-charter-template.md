### Deadlines and Accountability

**Internal deadlines are binding.** Missing an internal deadline affects everyone. If you anticipate missing a deadline:
1. Notify the group at least {{advance_notice}} days before the deadline
2. Provide a revised completion date
3. If the delay exceeds {{max_delay}} days, the group will discuss redistribution

**What happens if someone misses a deadline without notice:**
1. First occurrence: The group discusses the issue at the next meeting. The member provides a revised timeline.
2. Second occurrence: The group holds a formal check-in using the conflict resolution framework. The issue is documented.
3. Third occurrence: The group contacts the module tutor for guidance. The issue is recorded in the contribution statement.

### Conflict Resolution

**Step 1 — Direct conversation.** If you have a concern, raise it directly with the person involved. Use specific, factual language: "I noticed that Section 3 has not been submitted and the deadline was Tuesday" rather than "You never do your work."

**Step 2 — Group discussion.** If the direct conversation does not resolve the issue, raise it at the next group meeting. The group discusses the issue together and agrees on a resolution.

**Step 3 — External mediation.** If the group cannot resolve the issue, contact {{escalation_contact}} (e.g., the module tutor, personal tutor, or student support services) for guidance.

**Ground rules for difficult conversations:**
- Focus on the work, not the person
- Listen to understand, not to respond
- Acknowledge when someone has a valid point
- Agree on specific next steps, not vague commitments
